(ZHANG, Zaikun)

Postdoctoral Researcher, Department of Mathematics, University of Coimbra

Education

  • Sept. 2007 – July 2012, Ph.D., Institute of Computational Mathematics and Scientific/Engineering Computing, Chinese Academy of Sciences, supervised by Professor Ya-Xiang Yuan.

  • May 2010 – Aug. 2010, visiting student, Institute for Computer Science, University of Bayreuth, Germany, funded by Humboldt Foundation, visiting Professor Klaus Schittkowski.

  • Sept. 2003 – July 2007, B.Sc., College of Mathematics, Jilin University, China.

Position

  • Sept. 2012 – present, postdoctoral researcher, in the group of Professor Luís Nunes Vicente, at Department of Mathematics, University of Coimbra, funded by FCT grant PTDC/MAT/116736/2010.

Research Interests

  • Nonlinear optimization and its applications.

  • Derivative-free optimization methods (methods that do not use derivatives).

  • I work to develop new algorithms and analyze the theoretical properties, including global convergence, local convergence rate, and worst-case complexity.

  • Zaikun ZHANG, On Derivative-Free Optimization Methods (in Chinese, but with an English abstract), Ph.D. Thesis, Institute of Computational Mathematics and Scientific/Engineering Computing, Chinese Academy of Sciences, Beijing, 2012

  • Zaikun ZHANG, Notes on the Sobolev (Semi)Norms of Quadratic Functions, 2011
